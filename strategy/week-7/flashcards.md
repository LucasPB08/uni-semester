What is administrative heritage?	An organization's internal capabilities that may constrain its ability to respond to environmental changes (Bartlett & Ghoshal)
What are the three strategic objectives in Ghoshal's Globalization Framework?	1) Achieving efficiency in current operations, 2) Managing risks, 3) Innovation, learning, and adaptation
What are the three sources of competitive advantage in Ghoshal's Framework?	1) National differences, 2) Scale economies, 3) Scope economies
What are the four types of international strategy?	1) Multidomestic, 2) International, 3) Global, 4) Transnational
What characterizes a multidomestic strategy?	High local responsiveness, low global integration; products/services adapted to each national market
What characterizes a global strategy?	High global integration, low local responsiveness; standardized products/services worldwide
What characterizes a transnational strategy?	Simultaneously achieves global efficiency, national responsiveness, AND worldwide leveraging of innovations and learning
Why did the transnational strategy emerge?	Recognition that matching capabilities to a single environment no longer holds—neither multidomestic, international, nor global strategy alone is sufficient
What are the four attributes of Porter's Diamond of National Advantage?	1) Factor conditions, 2) Demand conditions, 3) Related and supporting industries, 4) Firm strategy, structure, and rivalry
What are factor conditions in Porter's Diamond?	A nation's position in factors of production such as skilled labor and infrastructure
What are demand conditions in Porter's Diamond?	The nature of home-market demand for the product or service
What is the Double Diamond Framework?	Rugman's extension of Porter's Diamond arguing that smaller nations' competitive advantage may derive from attributes in their larger foreign diamond
What two questions determine entry mode strategy?	1) What level of resource commitment is the organization prepared to make? 2) What level of control over international operations does it require?
What determines the answers to entry mode questions?	The organization's perception of international risk
What was Theodore Levitt's view on globalization?	Consumer preferences are "irrevocably homogenized" — advocates for global standardization
How did Douglas and Wind counter Levitt's view?	While global segments with similar needs exist, this is NOT a universal trend; important national differences persist
What two types of factors motivate international expansion?	1) Organizational factors (within the firm), 2) Environmental factors (outside the firm's control)
What three things must a transnational organization simultaneously achieve?	1) Global efficiency, 2) National responsiveness, 3) Worldwide leveraging of innovations and learning
