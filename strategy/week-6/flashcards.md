What is the key difference between management and leadership?	Management is about coping with complexity; Leadership is about dealing with change
Why do well-formulated strategies often fail?	They fail due to poor implementation - strategies must be effectively communicated, properly resourced, and accepted by individuals who implement them
What are the 5 types of organizational structures?	1) Simple, 2) Functional, 3) Divisional, 4) Matrix, 5) Network
What is the specialization-coordination trade-off?	Organizations must balance specialization (efficiency through division of labor) with coordination (ensuring parts work together effectively)
Why has the matrix structure been "found wanting"?	It violates unity of command, creates complex interactions, and has proved costly and burdensome
What do Ghoshal and Bartlett advocate instead of matrix structures?	Focus on organizational processes and a more integrative approach, rather than merely making structural changes
What is a strategic control system?	Mechanisms that allow strategy to be evaluated against stated aims and allow for changes if necessary
What tension exists in strategic control systems?	Balancing the need for managers to work toward a common goal while allowing them to develop innovative solutions without constraints
What two factors does Goold and Quinn's contingency framework compare?	Environmental turbulence vs. the ability of senior managers to state and measure strategic objectives
What are the two types of strategic change?	1) Evolutionary change - gradual, incremental adjustments; 2) Revolutionary change - dramatic, discontinuous transformation
What does Greiner's model address?	The challenges facing managers of growing organizations as they evolve through stages, each ending in a crisis that must be resolved
What are the 5 stages and crises in Greiner's Growth Model?	1) Creativity → Leadership crisis, 2) Direction → Autonomy crisis, 3) Delegation → Control crisis, 4) Coordination → Red Tape crisis, 5) Collaboration → Internal growth limits
What is the key insight of Greiner's model?	What makes you successful in one stage becomes your problem in the next - the solution to each crisis plants the seeds of the next crisis
What is a BHAG?	Big Hairy Audacious Goal - used by visionary organizations to guide their onward progress
What is a learning organization?	An organization in which people continually learn and are allowed to challenge existing mental models
Why is emotional intelligence important for leaders?	Emotional intelligence is a better predictor of leader success than either IQ or technical skills
What are the benefits and dangers of narcissistic leaders?	Benefits: Bold vision, decisive action, may be beneficial in turbulent markets. Dangers: May not listen to others, can be destructive to organizations
What is Theory E in strategic change?	Change focused on economic value, top-down driven, emphasizing structures, systems, and shareholder value
What is Theory O in strategic change?	Change focused on organizational capability, participative, emphasizing culture and employee development
Why should organizations combine Theory E and Theory O?	To simultaneously achieve lasting change AND an increase in shareholder value - neither alone is sufficient
What is the leader's role in strategy implementation?	Create an organizational culture that empowers managers to respond to opportunities and innovate without fear of reprisals
Why might speed of organizational learning be a competitive advantage?	Organizations that learn faster can adapt more quickly to changes, creating sustainable competitive advantage (Stalk 1988)
