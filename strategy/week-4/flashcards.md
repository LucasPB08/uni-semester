What is the difference between shareholder maximization and stakeholder approach?	Shareholder maximization: purpose is to add value for shareholders. Stakeholder approach: corporations must also account for wider societal needs to grow responsibly and sustainably.
What is Economic Value Added (EVA)?	A performance measure that focuses on value creation for shareholders by comparing economic profit (not just accounting profit) to capital employed.
What is the Balanced Scorecard?	A performance assessment tool measuring four perspectives: financial, customer, internal business processes, and learning & growth. Includes stakeholders, not just financial data.
What is benchmarking?	Comparing organizational performance against competitors or best-in-class firms (even outside your industry) to identify improvement opportunities.
What is the difference between corporate strategy and business strategy?	Corporate strategy: "What businesses should we compete in?" Business strategy: "How do we compete in our chosen industry/market?"
What are Porter's three generic competitive strategies?	1) Cost leadership - lowest cost producer, 2) Differentiation - unique value perceived by customers, 3) Focus - target a specific market niche
What does "stuck in the middle" mean in Porter's framework?	When an organization fails to clearly pursue one generic strategy, leading to poor performance because it lacks a clear competitive advantage.
What is the Resource-Based View (RBV)?	An approach to strategy that focuses on matching internal resources and capabilities to external environment needs. Profit derives from BOTH industry attractiveness AND internal capabilities.
What are the four stages of the industry life-cycle?	1) Introduction, 2) Growth, 3) Maturity, 4) Decline
What are the four competitive environments in turbulent markets?	Equilibrium, fluctuating equilibrium, punctuated equilibrium, and disequilibrium.
What is synergy in corporate strategy?	When total output from combining businesses is greater than the sum of output if businesses operated independently.
What are the four growth strategies (Ansoff Matrix)?	1) Market penetration (existing products/existing markets), 2) Product development (new products/existing markets), 3) Market development (existing products/new markets), 4) Diversification (new products/new markets)
What is the difference between related and unrelated diversification?	Related: expanding into businesses with connections/similarities (vertical or horizontal integration). Unrelated: expanding into businesses with no clear connections to existing activities.
What are the four methods to implement corporate strategies?	1) Internal developments, 2) Mergers and acquisitions, 3) Joint ventures, 4) Strategic alliances
What is the corporate parenting approach?	Parent company uses its value creation insights to identify a "heartland" of businesses where it can add substantial value. Value test: Is value added greater than if business units operated independently?
What is the BCG Portfolio Matrix used for?	Evaluating and categorizing business units based on market growth rate and relative market share to help allocate resources across the portfolio.
What is the difference between SWOT and TOWS?	SWOT is analytical (lists factors). TOWS is action-oriented (generates strategic options by systematically matching internal and external factors).
What are SO strategies in TOWS?	"Maxi-Maxi" strategies: Use internal strengths to exploit external opportunities. Most aggressive/growth-oriented.
What are WO strategies in TOWS?	"Mini-Maxi" strategies: Overcome internal weaknesses by exploiting external opportunities. Often involves acquiring new resources or capabilities.
What are ST strategies in TOWS?	"Maxi-Mini" strategies: Use internal strengths to mitigate external threats. Defensive strategies leveraging competitive advantages.
What are WT strategies in TOWS?	"Mini-Mini" strategies: Minimize weaknesses and avoid threats. Most defensive - may involve retrenchment, divestment, or exit.
