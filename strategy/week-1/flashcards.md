# Week 1 Flashcards - Strategy Course

## Quizlet Import Format (Tab-Separated)

What is competitive advantage?	That which differentiates an organization from its competitors in the eyes of customers
What is strategic positioning according to Porter?	Deliberately choosing a different set of activities to deliver a unique mix of value
What are Porter's three strategic questions?	1) Who should we target as customers? 2) What products/services should we offer? 3) How can we do this efficiently?
What are the three components of the strategic management process?	1) Strategy Analysis, 2) Strategy Formulation, 3) Strategy Implementation (all are co-dependent)
What is strategy analysis?	Analyzing the external environment (general + competitive) and internal environment (resources, capabilities, strengths/weaknesses) to understand where the organization is and where it wants to go
What is strategy formulation?	The process of choosing between strategic options using both analytical thinking and creative insight
Why is strategy implementation critical?	Strategy without implementation is tantamount to not having a strategy at all
What is SWOT analysis?	A framework analyzing Strengths (internal), Weaknesses (internal), Opportunities (external), and Threats (external) to match capabilities with market conditions
What is an intended strategy?	A strategy that the organization has explicitly chosen to pursue and worked out in detail
What is an emergent strategy?	A strategy that develops gradually through learning and adaptation to meet the needs of the external environment
What is a realized strategy?	The strategy that the organization actually carries out in practice
What is the difference between vision and mission?	Vision = why the organization exists (desired future state); Mission = what business we are in (communicated to outside world)
What is Drucker's "theory of business"?	The assumptions about markets, customers, and capabilities that guide an organization; every theory eventually becomes obsolete and must be questioned periodically
What are the three types of strategy?	1) Corporate strategy (which industries to compete in), 2) Business strategy (how to compete in chosen market), 3) Functional strategy (how functions support business strategy)
What are the five components of emotional intelligence (Goleman)?	1) Self-awareness, 2) Self-regulation, 3) Motivation, 4) Empathy, 5) Social skills
Why is emotional intelligence important for leaders?	It is twice as important as technical skills and IQ for outstanding leadership performance at all organizational levels
What characterizes productive narcissistic leaders?	Visionary, sensitive to big picture, skilled at communicating vision, able to galvanize change (e.g., Jack Welch, Bill Gates)
What are the dangers of destructive narcissistic leadership?	Leaders become over-sensitive to criticism, poor listeners, suspicious, arrogant, and ignore those who disagree with their vision
What are the three types of leadership skills (Groysberg)?	1) General management skills (highly portable), 2) Company-specific skills (rarely portable), 3) Industry/relationship skills (moderately portable)
What key insight explains why some executives succeed when changing companies?	Success depends on the FIT between the executive's skill portfolio and the new organization's strategic needs
