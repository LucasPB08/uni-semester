# Week 2 Exercises: Time Value of Money

## Instructions
- Work through these problems without looking at the solutions
- Show your work and formulas
- Use a calculator for complex calculations
- Check your answers against solutions.md when done

---

## Section 1: Future Value (Basic)

**Exercise 1.1**
You invest $2,500 at an annual interest rate of 7%. How much will you have after 4 years?

**Exercise 1.2**
Your savings account has $800 earning 3% annual interest. What will the balance be after 10 years?

**Exercise 1.3**
You invest $5,000 at 9% annual interest. How long will it take to double your money?

---

## Section 2: Present Value (Basic)

**Exercise 2.1**
You will receive $10,000 in 5 years. If the discount rate is 6%, what is it worth today?

**Exercise 2.2**
A bond will pay you $1,000 in 8 years. The appropriate discount rate is 4%. What is the present value?

**Exercise 2.3**
Your uncle promises to give you $15,000 in 3 years. If you could earn 8% on your investments, what is this promise worth today?

---

## Section 3: Annuities

**Exercise 3.1**
You will receive $500 per year for 6 years, starting one year from now. If the discount rate is 7%, what is the present value of this annuity?

**Exercise 3.2**
You plan to save $2,000 per year for 10 years in an account earning 5% annual interest. How much will you have at the end of 10 years (future value)?

**Exercise 3.3**
A car loan requires monthly payments of $400 for 5 years (60 months). If the monthly interest rate is 0.5%, what is the present value (loan amount)?

---

## Section 4: Perpetuities

**Exercise 4.1**
A perpetuity pays $300 per year forever. If the discount rate is 6%, what is its present value?

**Exercise 4.2**
You want to endow a scholarship that pays $5,000 per year forever. If the expected return is 4%, how much must you donate today?

**Exercise 4.3**
A preferred stock pays a constant dividend of $2.50 per share forever. If investors require a 5% return, what is the stock worth?

---

## Section 5: Finding Interest Rates

**Exercise 5.1**
You invest $3,000 and it grows to $4,500 in 6 years. What was the annual interest rate?

**Exercise 5.2**
An investment doubles in 9 years. What is the annual interest rate?

**Exercise 5.3**
You invest $1,000 today and receive $1,331 in 3 years. What is the annual return?

---

## Section 6: Real vs Nominal Interest Rates

**Exercise 6.1**
The nominal interest rate is 8% and the inflation rate is 2.5%. What is the real interest rate?

**Exercise 6.2**
You expect to earn a real return of 5% per year. If inflation is 3%, what nominal interest rate should you target?

**Exercise 6.3**
A bond promises to pay $1,000 in one year. The nominal interest rate is 10% and expected inflation is 4%. What is the present value in real terms?

---

## Section 7: APR vs EAR

**Exercise 7.1**
A bank offers 6% APR with monthly compounding. What is the effective annual rate (EAR)?

**Exercise 7.2**
A credit card charges 2% per month. What are the APR and EAR?

**Exercise 7.3**
Which is better: 8% APR compounded quarterly, or 7.9% APR compounded monthly?

---

## Section 8: Mixed Problems (Challenge)

**Exercise 8.1**
You need $50,000 in 7 years for a down payment on a house. If you can earn 6% annually, how much must you invest today?

**Exercise 8.2**
A lottery offers you two options:
- Option A: $100,000 today
- Option B: $20,000 per year for 6 years (starting in 1 year)

If the discount rate is 8%, which option is better?

**Exercise 8.3**
You take out a $200,000 mortgage at 5% annual interest (0.4167% monthly) for 30 years (360 months). What is your monthly payment? (Hint: This is an annuity problem - solve for the payment amount C.)

**Exercise 8.4**
A project requires an investment of $100,000 today and will return $25,000 per year for 6 years. If your required return is 10%, should you invest? (Calculate the present value of returns and compare to cost.)

**Exercise 8.5**
You invest $5,000 at a nominal rate of 7% per year. Inflation averages 2% per year. How much purchasing power (in today's dollars) will you have in 10 years?

---

## Bonus Challenge

**Exercise 9.1**
A perpetuity-due (payments start immediately, not in 1 year) pays $1,000 per year. If the discount rate is 5%, what is its present value? (Hint: Think about the timing difference from a regular perpetuity.)

**Exercise 9.2**
You plan to retire in 40 years. You want to withdraw $50,000 per year for 30 years during retirement (first withdrawal at retirement). If the interest rate is 6%, how much do you need to save each year for the next 40 years?
